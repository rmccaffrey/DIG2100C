Places I want to visit

Glacier National Park

I spent some of my formative years growing up in Wyoming and Montana, which gave me a chance to visit several state and national parks. I've seen Yellowstone, Red Lodge, Devil's Tower, the Little Bighorn Battlefield, and Mount Rushmore.

One place I never made it is Glacier National Park, located in north-west Montana. I've wanted for a long time to visit its mountains and see its population of large wildlife: moose, elk and grizzly bear. As the park website puts it:

Come and experience Glacier's pristine forests, alpine meadows, rugged mountains, and spectacular lakes. With over 700 miles of trails, Glacier is a hiker's paradise for adventurous visitors seeking wilderness and solitude.

I would love to go and do nothing for two weeks but eat well, sleep early, and walk in the mountains all day long.

What I plan to bring

Since this would be a hiking trip in the vicinity of bears, and since I'm going to want to remember my stay at Glacier for the rest of my life, here is what I plan to bring for the trip.

  • walking stick
  • bear bell & bear spray
  • my Canon AE1 35mm film camera
  • my Nikon point and shoot digital camera
